# DSA Study Plan for Top MNCs

This study plan is designed to help you refresh your Data Structures and Algorithms knowledge and prepare for technical interviews at Google and other top tech companies. It's structured to progress from fundamentals to advanced topics over approximately 12 weeks, but you can adjust the pace based on your comfort level.

## Phase 4: Consistent Practice & Mocks (Weeks 11+)

This phase is about consolidating your knowledge by solving a wide variety of problems and simulating interview conditions.

*   **Practice LeetCode Medium/Hards**
    *   **Goal:** Solidify pattern recognition and improve problem-solving speed and accuracy.
    *   **Strategy:**
        *   Use a curated problem list like the [NeetCode 150](https://neetcode.io/practice) or the [Blind 75](https://www.teamblind.com/post/New-Year-Gift---Curated-List-of-Top-75-LeetCode-Questions-to-Save-Your-Time-OaM1orEU).
        *   Focus on solving problems from company-specific lists for your target companies.
        *   When you get stuck, spend at least 30-45 minutes struggling before looking at a hint or solution.
        *   When you do look at a solution, understand it deeply. Then, try to re-implement it yourself without looking. Revisit the problem a few days later.

*   **Mock Interviews**
    *   **Goal:** Simulate the high-pressure interview environment and get feedback on your communication and problem-solving process.
    *   **Strategy:**
        *   **Pramp:** A free peer-to-peer mock interview platform. Excellent for getting lots of practice.
        *   **Interviewing.io:** Provides anonymous technical mock interviews with engineers from top companies. You can often get very high-quality feedback.
        *   **Peers:** Practice with friends or colleagues who are also preparing.
    *   **Focus Areas:**
        *   Clearly explain your thought process *as you're solving the problem*.
        *   Discuss trade-offs between different approaches.
        *   Write clean, modular, and bug-free code.
        *   Test your code with edge cases.

*   **Review and Refine**
    *   **Goal:** Identify weak areas and continuously improve.
    *   **Strategy:**
        *   Keep a log of problems you've solved, especially the ones you found difficult. Note down the key insight or pattern you missed.
        *   Periodically re-solve problems from topics you covered in earlier weeks.
        *   Review common data structure implementations and their complexities until they become second nature.

## General Tips for Success

*   **Consistency is Key:** Aim to solve 2-4 problems every day.
*   **Focus on Patterns:** Don't just memorize solutions. Understand the underlying pattern so you can apply it to new problems.
*   **Complexity Analysis:** For every problem, analyze the time and space complexity of your solution.
*   **Spaced Repetition:** Revisit topics and problems from previous weeks to ensure the concepts stick.
*   **Articulate Your Thoughts:** Practice explaining your thought process out loud as you solve a problem. This is crucial for interviews.
