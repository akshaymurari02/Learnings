# DSA Study Plan for Top MNCs

Welcome to your personalized DSA study plan! This guide is designed to help you refresh your knowledge and master the key patterns needed for technical interviews at Google and other top tech companies. Given your background, we'll focus on a structured recap and intensive practice.

## Study Strategy

1.  **Understand the Core Concept:** Before jumping into problems, make sure you have a solid grasp of the data structure or algorithm. Watch a video, read an article, and understand the time/space complexity of the main operations.
2.  **Start with Easy Problems:** Begin with a few easy-tagged problems on platforms like LeetCode to build confidence and solidify your understanding.
3.  **Move to Medium/Hard:** This is where you'll spend most of your time. These problems are representative of what you'll encounter in interviews. Don't be discouraged if you get stuck.
4.  **The 45-Minute Rule:** If you can't solve a problem within 45 minutes, look at the solution. Understand it thoroughly, then try to implement it yourself without looking.
5.  **Spaced Repetition:** Revisit problems you've solved after a few days, then a week, then a month to ensure the concepts stick.
6.  **Track Your Progress:** Use a spreadsheet or the LeetCode/NeetCode platforms to track the problems you've solved and the patterns you've covered.

### 17. Further Topics & Conclusion

The topics below are less common in general interviews but can appear, especially in competitive programming contexts or at companies like Google.

*   **String Matching (KMP/Rabin Karp):** For efficient substring searching.
*   **Topological Sort:** Covered under Graphs, but important to know as a standalone concept.
*   **Segment Tree / Fenwick Tree:** For efficient range queries and updates.
*   **Math & Number Theory:** Prime numbers, modular arithmetic, etc.
*   **Game Theory / Minimax:** For problems involving optimal strategy in games.

**Final Advice:**

*   **Consistency is Key:** A little bit of practice every day is more effective than cramming.
*   **Mock Interviews:** Practice explaining your thought process out loud. This is a critical skill.
*   **Behavioral Questions:** Don't neglect these! Prepare stories using the STAR method.
*   **System Design:** If you have 2+ years of experience, you will be asked system design questions. This requires separate, dedicated preparation.

Good luck with your preparation! You've got this.
